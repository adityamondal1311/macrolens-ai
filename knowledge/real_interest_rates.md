# Real Interest Rates

## What They Are
Real interest rates are nominal interest rates adjusted for inflation. They represent the true cost of borrowing and the true return on lending in terms of purchasing power.

```
Real Rate = Nominal Rate - Expected Inflation
```

Example: If the 10Y Treasury yields 4.5% and 10Y breakeven inflation is 2.3%, the real yield is 2.2%. An investor earns 2.2% above inflation — a genuine return.

If the 10Y yields 1% and inflation expectations are 3%, the real yield is -2%. The investor loses 2% of purchasing power per year in real terms.

## Why Real Rates Are More Important Than Nominal Rates
Nominal rates tell you the number. Real rates tell you the economic reality.

A 5% interest rate with 6% inflation is actually stimulative — borrowers are being paid to borrow in real terms. A 2% rate with 0% inflation is moderately restrictive. The Fed understands this — their goal is to set real rates at the appropriate level, not just nominal rates.

## Real Rates and Gold
The most reliable relationship in all of macro finance:

**Real rates and gold are inversely correlated.**

- When real rates fall (especially into negative territory), gold rallies strongly
- When real rates rise, gold sells off

The logic: Gold has no yield. When real yields are negative, there is no opportunity cost to holding gold — bonds also lose purchasing power. When real yields are positive, investors prefer interest-bearing assets over gold.

This is why gold surged during 2020-2021 when the Fed held rates near zero while inflation rose (real rates went deeply negative), and sold off sharply in 2022 when the Fed hiked aggressively (real rates surged from -1% to +2%).

## Real Rates and Equity Valuations
Real rates are a key driver of equity market multiples (P/E ratios).

**Low real rates → High equity multiples**
- Lower discount rate → Future earnings worth more today
- Investors forced into equities (TINA — There Is No Alternative to stocks)
- Growth stocks with distant cash flows benefit most

**High real rates → Low equity multiples**
- Higher discount rate → Future earnings worth less today
- Bonds become attractive relative to equities
- Growth stocks de-rate sharply; value stocks relatively outperform

The 2022 equity bear market was primarily a real rate shock — the 10Y real yield went from -1% to +1.5% in months, causing enormous P/E compression especially in tech and growth.

## The TINA Trade
"There Is No Alternative" — when real rates are deeply negative, investors have no choice but to hold equities and risk assets to preserve purchasing power. This dynamic artificially inflates equity valuations during periods of extreme monetary easing.

When real rates normalize, TINA disappears and equities reprice lower to reflect the available alternatives (bonds now offering real positive returns).

## Measuring Real Rates
**TIPS Yields:** The most direct market measure. 10Y TIPS yield = 10Y real rate.

**Implied Real Rates:**
```
Implied Real Rate = Nominal Treasury Yield - Breakeven Inflation
```

The Fed publishes real rate estimates through its H.15 data series.

## The Neutral Real Rate (R*)
The theoretical real interest rate consistent with the economy at full employment and stable inflation — neither stimulative nor restrictive. Estimated at roughly 0.5-1.0% in the US currently (though debate exists).

When real rates are above R*: monetary policy is restrictive, slowing the economy.
When real rates are below R*: monetary policy is stimulative, accelerating the economy.

## Real Rates Across Countries
Real rate differentials drive currency movements. If US real rates are 2% and European real rates are 0%, capital flows to the US, strengthening the dollar and weakening the euro. This is why the dollar surged in 2022 — US real rates rose faster than elsewhere.

## Bottom Line
Real rates are the master variable in macro. They drive gold, equity valuations, currency flows, and credit conditions. Watch the 10Y TIPS yield daily. When it moves sharply, every other asset class responds.
