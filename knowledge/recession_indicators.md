# Recession Indicators

## What Is a Recession?
Technically defined as two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth. The NBER (National Bureau of Economic Research) is the official arbiter in the US and uses a broader definition including employment, income, and industrial production.

## Leading Indicators (Predict Recessions Before They Happen)

### 1. Yield Curve Inversion
The most reliable recession predictor. When the 2Y Treasury yield exceeds the 10Y yield, a recession has followed within 6-24 months in every instance since the 1950s (with one false positive in the mid-1960s).

The 3M/10Y spread (used by the NY Fed's recession probability model) has an even better track record. The deeper and longer the inversion, the more severe the eventual recession.

### 2. PMI (Purchasing Managers Index)
Monthly survey of corporate purchasing managers. Key threshold: **50**
- Above 50 = expansion
- Below 50 = contraction

ISM Manufacturing PMI below 48 for several months is a strong recession warning. Services PMI is watched alongside manufacturing.

### 3. Conference Board Leading Economic Index (LEI)
A composite of 10 leading indicators including building permits, stock prices, consumer expectations, manufacturing orders, and credit conditions. When LEI falls for several consecutive months, recession risk is high.

### 4. Credit Spreads
The difference between corporate bond yields and Treasury yields. When credit spreads widen sharply, it signals stress in the financial system and tightening credit conditions — a classic recession precursor.
- **Investment Grade Spreads:** OAS (option-adjusted spread) over Treasuries
- **High Yield Spreads:** HY spreads above 700-800bps historically signals recession territory

### 5. Unemployment Claims
Initial jobless claims are a high-frequency (weekly) recession indicator. A sustained rise in claims signals labor market deterioration. The Sahm Rule: when the 3-month average unemployment rate rises 0.5% above the prior 12-month low, the US is in or near recession.

### 6. Housing Starts and Building Permits
Housing is highly interest rate sensitive and leads the economic cycle. Sharp drops in housing starts often precede recessions by 6-12 months.

### 7. Consumer Confidence
The Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index and University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment. Sharp drops in confidence reduce spending, creating self-fulfilling recessionary dynamics.

## Coincident Indicators (Confirm Current State)

### GDP Growth
Released quarterly with a lag. By the time two negative quarters are confirmed, the recession is well underway. Real-time GDP trackers (like Atlanta Fed GDPNow) provide more timely estimates.

### Industrial Production
Monthly measure of factory, mining, and utility output. Declining industrial production confirms economic contraction.

### Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)
Monthly jobs report. The most important US economic data release. During recessions, payrolls turn negative. But employment is a lagging indicator — firms stop hiring before they start firing.

## Lagging Indicators (Confirm Recessions After the Fact)

### Unemployment Rate
Peaks after recessions have already ended. The Fed's dual mandate makes it critical for policy — but it confirms rather than predicts.

### Corporate Earnings
Earnings fall during recessions but the stock market typically bottoms 6 months before the recession ends, anticipating the recovery.

## Recession Playbook for Macro Traders

**Before Recession (Late Cycle):**
- Reduce equity exposure, especially cyclicals
- Buy long-duration Treasuries (rates will fall)
- Buy gold
- Reduce high-yield credit exposure
- Short cyclical currencies (AUD, CAD, EM FX)
- Long USD, JPY, CHF

**During Recession:**
- Long Treasuries (maximum duration)
- Long gold
- Long defensive equities (utilities, healthcare, consumer staples)
- Short cyclicals, financials

**Recovery:**
- Rotate back into equities (cyclicals first)
- Long commodities
- Long EM assets
- Short Treasuries (yields will rise)

## Bottom Line
No single indicator predicts recessions perfectly. Macro traders build a "weight of evidence" approach — when the yield curve, PMI, credit spreads, and leading indicators all point the same direction, conviction is highest. The yield curve remains the single most reliable individual indicator.
