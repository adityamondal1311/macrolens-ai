# Yield Curve

## What It Is
The yield curve plots interest rates of bonds with equal credit quality but different maturities — typically US Treasuries from 1 month to 30 years. Its shape is one of the most powerful macro signals in finance.

## Key Yield Curve Spreads Traders Watch
- **2Y10Y Spread:** 10-year yield minus 2-year yield. The most watched spread on Wall Street.
- **3M10Y Spread:** 10-year yield minus 3-month T-bill. The NY Fed uses this for recession probability models.
- **5Y30Y Spread:** Measures the steepness of the long end of the curve.

## Yield Curve Shapes

### Normal (Upward Sloping)
Long-term yields are higher than short-term yields. This is the natural state — investors demand more compensation for lending money over longer periods. Signals healthy economic growth expectations.

### Flat
Short and long-term yields are similar. Signals uncertainty about future growth. Often a transition phase between normal and inverted.

### Inverted
Short-term yields are higher than long-term yields. This happens when the Fed has raised short-term rates aggressively while markets expect future rate cuts (implying a coming recession or slowdown).

### Humped (Butterfly)
Medium-term yields are higher than both short and long-term yields. Rare, signals complex market expectations.

## Why Yield Curve Inversion Predicts Recession
The yield curve has inverted before every US recession since the 1950s with only one false positive. The mechanism:

1. Fed raises short-term rates to fight inflation
2. Higher short-term rates slow the economy
3. Markets anticipate a slowdown and future rate cuts
4. Long-term yields fall below short-term yields
5. Bank profitability collapses (banks borrow short, lend long — inversion kills their margin)
6. Credit tightens, investment slows, recession follows

The average lag between inversion and recession is 12-18 months, which is why traders watch it closely but don't panic immediately.

## Curve Steepening vs Flattening Trades

### Bull Steepener
Long end yields fall faster than short end. Happens during recessions when Fed cuts rates. Long bonds outperform.

### Bear Steepener
Long end yields rise faster than short end. Happens when inflation expectations rise. Dangerous for equity valuations.

### Bull Flattener
Short end yields fall faster than long end. Happens when Fed signals rate cuts are coming.

### Bear Flattener
Short end yields rise faster than long end. Happens when Fed is actively hiking. Classic late-cycle signal.

## Trading the Yield Curve
- **Curve steepener trade:** Long 2Y Treasuries, Short 10Y Treasuries (or via futures)
- **Curve flattener trade:** Short 2Y Treasuries, Long 10Y Treasuries
- These are duration-neutral trades — profit comes from the change in spread, not the level of rates

## Current Relevance
The 2022-2023 inversion was the deepest since the 1980s, with the 2Y10Y spread reaching -108 basis points. This followed the most aggressive Fed hiking cycle in 40 years.

## Bottom Line
An inverted yield curve is the most reliable recession predictor in macro. Watch the 2Y10Y spread daily. Inversion doesn't mean sell everything immediately — the lag matters. Position sizing and timing are everything.
