# Macro Trading Strategies

## What Is Macro Trading?
Global macro is an investment strategy that makes bets based on macroeconomic and geopolitical analysis across countries and asset classes. Macro traders take positions in currencies, bonds, equities, and commodities based on their view of the global economic cycle.

Famous macro traders include George Soros, Stanley Druckenmiller, Ray Dalio, and Paul Tudor Jones.

## Core Macro Trading Frameworks

### The Economic Cycle Framework
The economy moves through predictable phases, each favoring different assets:

**Early Cycle (Recovery)**
- Growth accelerating from low base
- Fed still accommodative
- Best assets: Equities (cyclicals), corporate credit, EM assets
- Worst assets: Cash, short-duration bonds

**Mid Cycle (Expansion)**
- Steady growth, moderate inflation
- Fed gradually tightening
- Best assets: Equities (broad), commodities
- Worst assets: Long-duration bonds

**Late Cycle (Overheating)**
- Growth peaking, inflation rising
- Fed aggressively hiking
- Best assets: Commodities, TIPS, short-duration bonds, cash
- Worst assets: Long bonds, high-multiple growth stocks

**Recession**
- Growth contracting
- Fed cutting rates
- Best assets: Long-duration Treasuries, gold, defensive equities
- Worst assets: Cyclicals, high-yield credit, EM assets

### The Inflation Matrix
Four quadrants based on growth and inflation direction:

| | Inflation Rising | Inflation Falling |
|---|---|---|
| **Growth Rising** | Commodities, TIPS | Equities, Credit |
| **Growth Falling** | Stagflation hedge (gold, commodities) | Long bonds, cash |

### Risk-On / Risk-Off (RORO)
Markets oscillate between periods of risk appetite (risk-on) and risk aversion (risk-off).

**Risk-On indicators:**
- Equities rising
- High-yield spreads tightening
- EM currencies strengthening
- VIX falling
- USD weakening

**Risk-Off indicators:**
- Equities falling
- Flight to Treasuries (yields fall)
- USD, JPY, CHF strengthening
- VIX spiking
- Gold rising

## Key Macro Trades

### The Rates Trade
Taking a position on the direction of interest rates. If you expect the Fed to hike more than markets price, you short bonds (rates go up, bond prices go down). Position via Treasury futures, interest rate swaps, or bond ETFs.

### The Curve Trade
Betting on the shape of the yield curve steepening or flattening. Duration-neutral: long 2Y, short 10Y (flattener) or vice versa (steepener).

### The Carry Trade
Borrow low-rate currency, invest in high-rate currency. Works in calm markets; unwinds violently in risk-off.

### The Dollar Trade
DXY positioning based on relative growth and rate differentials between the US and the rest of the world. Long dollar when Fed is tightening relative to other central banks.

### The Commodity Macro Trade
Long commodities when: global growth is strong, dollar is weak, supply is constrained. Oil, copper, and gold each have different demand drivers.

### The EM Trade
Long EM assets when: dollar is weak, global growth is strong, risk appetite is high, commodity prices are rising. Short EM when the opposite holds.

## Key Macro Indicators to Watch

### Leading Indicators (Predict the future)
- PMI (Purchasing Managers Index) — above 50 = expansion
- Yield curve shape
- Conference Board LEI
- ISM Manufacturing

### Coincident Indicators (Confirm current state)
- GDP growth
- Industrial production
- Employment

### Lagging Indicators (Confirm past trends)
- Unemployment rate
- CPI
- Corporate earnings

## Risk Management in Macro Trading
Macro trades can be wrong for long periods before being right. Key principles:
- **Size positions based on conviction and volatility**, not just direction
- **Use options to define risk** — buy calls/puts to cap downside
- **Watch for crowded trades** — when everyone is on the same side, the unwind is violent
- **Correlations change in crises** — diversification can fail when you need it most

## Bottom Line
Macro trading is about identifying where the global economy is in its cycle, what central banks will do next, and which asset classes will benefit. The best macro traders combine rigorous analysis with strict risk management and the patience to wait for their thesis to play out.
