# Risk-On / Risk-Off (RORO)

## What It Is
Risk-on and risk-off describe two distinct market regimes that dictate how capital is allocated across global asset classes. In risk-on environments, investors embrace higher-yielding, higher-volatility assets. In risk-off environments, they flee to safety regardless of valuations.

Understanding which regime the market is in is foundational to macro positioning.

## What Causes Risk-On

### Positive Growth Signals
- Strong GDP, PMI, or employment data
- Earnings beats above consensus
- Positive economic surprises

### Accommodative Central Banks
- Fed cutting rates or signaling cuts
- QE programs expanding
- Central banks providing liquidity

### Geopolitical Calm
- Reduction of geopolitical tensions
- Trade deal announcements
- Diplomatic progress

### Falling Volatility
- Low VIX environment encourages risk-taking
- Tight credit spreads signal confidence
- Stable currency markets

## What Causes Risk-Off

### Recession Fears
- Weak economic data (PMI below 50, jobs miss)
- Yield curve inversion or deepening
- Leading indicators rolling over

### Central Bank Tightening
- Aggressive rate hikes
- QT accelerating
- Hawkish central bank communication

### Geopolitical Shocks
- Wars, terrorist attacks, political crises
- Sanctions, trade wars
- Election uncertainty

### Financial System Stress
- Bank failures or credit events
- Credit spread blowout
- Liquidity crises (repo market stress)

### Volatility Spikes
- VIX surging above 25-30
- Options markets pricing tail risk
- Cross-asset correlations converging to 1

## Asset Behavior in Risk-On

| Asset Class | Risk-On Behavior |
|---|---|
| Equities | Rally, cyclicals outperform defensives |
| High-Yield Bonds | Spreads tighten, prices rise |
| EM Equities | Outperform developed markets |
| EM Currencies | Appreciate vs USD |
| Commodity Currencies | AUD, CAD, NOK strengthen |
| US Dollar | Weakens (capital leaves safe haven) |
| JPY, CHF | Weaken (safe havens sold) |
| Gold | Mixed — can sell off if real rates rise |
| Volatility (VIX) | Falls |
| Treasury Bonds | Underperform (yields rise) |

## Asset Behavior in Risk-Off

| Asset Class | Risk-Off Behavior |
|---|---|
| Equities | Sell off, defensives outperform cyclicals |
| High-Yield Bonds | Spreads blow out, prices fall |
| EM Equities | Underperform sharply |
| EM Currencies | Depreciate vs USD |
| Commodity Currencies | AUD, CAD, NOK weaken |
| US Dollar | Strengthens (safe haven demand) |
| JPY, CHF | Strengthen sharply |
| Gold | Usually rallies (store of value) |
| Volatility (VIX) | Spikes |
| Treasury Bonds | Rally (flight to safety, yields fall) |

## The Safe Haven Hierarchy
In severe risk-off events, capital flows in a predictable order:
1. **US Treasuries** (ultimate safe haven)
2. **US Dollar** (reserve currency)
3. **Japanese Yen** (repatriation flows)
4. **Swiss Franc** (political neutrality, current account surplus)
5. **Gold** (store of value, no counterparty risk)

## Risk-Off Correlation Collapse
In severe risk-off events, correlations across asset classes converge toward 1. Everything falls together — equities, credit, commodities, EM — as forced selling and margin calls dominate. Diversification fails precisely when it's needed most.

This is why macro traders use options (tail hedges) rather than diversification alone to protect against risk-off events.

## Reading the RORO Regime
Key real-time indicators:
- **VIX level:** Below 15 = calm/risk-on; above 25 = stress; above 40 = crisis
- **High-yield spreads:** Tightening = risk-on; widening = risk-off
- **AUD/JPY:** Rising = risk-on; falling = risk-off
- **EM vs DM equity performance:** EM outperforming = risk-on
- **S&P 500 / US Treasury correlation:** Negative correlation = normal; positive = crisis regime

## RORO Regime Persistence
Risk-on regimes tend to persist for months to years. Risk-off episodes are typically shorter but more violent. Macro traders must distinguish between temporary risk-off corrections (buy the dip) and regime changes signaling sustained deterioration.

## Bottom Line
RORO is the daily language of macro markets. Before taking any position, ask: what regime are we in, what could change it, and how does my trade perform in both regimes? Positioning with the regime is far more important than individual security selection.
